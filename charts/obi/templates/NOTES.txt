OBI (OpenTelemetry eBPF Instrumentation) is now installed as a DaemonSet.

1. Check the pods are running on every node:

   kubectl --namespace {{ .Release.Namespace }} get pods -l "app={{ include "obi.name" . }}"

2. OBI is instrumenting these selectors:
{{- range .Values.discovery.instrument }}
   - {{ toYaml . | nindent 5 | trim }}
{{- end }}
{{- if not .Values.discovery.instrument }}

   WARNING: `discovery.instrument` is empty. OBI will instrument NO application
   processes and nothing will appear in Service Health. Set it, e.g.:

     discovery:
       instrument:
         - k8sNamespace: default
{{- end }}

3. OBI ships OTLP to: {{ .Values.otlpEndpoint }}

   Make sure a collector (the `otel-collector` or `alloy` chart) is reachable at
   that address and is forwarding to the ilert OTLP gateway. Without it, traces
   have nowhere to go.

4. Within a few minutes your services and their dependencies should appear on
   the ilert Service Health page.
